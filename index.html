WHY THIS BOOK

Because No One Talks About What Residency Really Costs

Beneath the white coat and the forced smile, the average residency looks nothing like what you signed up for. Sleep deprivation becomes a baseline. Immunity weakens. Anxiety climbs without acknowledgement. The sense of purpose that survived a drop year and cracked NEET — slowly begins to dull at the edges.

The problem is not you. It was never you. It is systemic, widespread, and almost entirely unspoken.

When Dr. Bhargude began speaking about residency on social media, hundreds of residents wrote in — unburdening themselves to a stranger on a screen because there was, it seemed, nowhere else to put it.

He looked for material written honestly for residents, by someone who had lived it from the inside. He found very little. So he built something of his own.

This book refuses the unspoken agreement that residency is supposed to be hard — and its casualties are supposed to be silent.

"Why not help people get the best out of something that is quietly taking the worst from them?"
— Dr. Devesh Bhargude

Excerpt: The First Shift

It was 2:00 AM on a Tuesday, and the emergency department was chaotic. The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sterile glare on the linoleum floor. I had been awake for twenty hours, fueled only by lukewarm coffee and pure adrenaline.

When you first walk into residency, you carry the weight of your medical degree like a shield. You think the letters 'MBBS' after your name will somehow protect you from the crushing reality of a failing healthcare system. You are wrong.

The transition from student to doctor happens in an instant. There is no grace period. One day you are observing, and the next, a nurse is looking at you, expecting orders for a patient whose blood pressure is plummeting.

This book isn't just about surviving those moments; it's about redefining them. It's about learning how to thrive when the environment demands your complete exhaustion. It's about taking back control of your residency life, on your own terms.

Every resident remembers their first solo night float. The terrifying realization that the buck stops with you. When the pager goes off, there is no attending to hide behind. It is a baptism by fire, and the only way out is through.

But amidst the chaos, there are moments of profound clarity. The first time you successfully revive a coding patient. The quiet gratitude in a family member's eyes. These are the fragments that you cling to, the pieces that build the foundation of your career.

ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Dr. Devesh Bhargude
DM Cardiology · MRCP UK · DNB · MD

Dr. Devesh Bhargude is a cardiologist in his final year of residency — and one of the most unconventional doctors you will meet. Coming from a small town of barely 2000 people, he built himself into someone who runs half marathons, deadlifts 200kg, reads 60+ books a year, trains martial arts at 4 AM, and still shows up for 36-hour ICU shifts.

He holds Top All India ranks, has presented at 10+ conferences, completed MRCP UK during residency, and actively shares evidence-based knowledge on nutrition, fitness, and medicine on Instagram.

But beyond the credentials — he meditates in the woods, nurtures deep family bonds, and believes the real growth of residency is mental and spiritual, not just clinical.

"Coming from a small town of 2000 people to becoming the version of me I always wanted to be — that is the real story residency made possible."
— Dr. Devesh Bhargude

A NOTE FROM THE AUTHOR

Thank You For
Choosing To Transform
Your Residency

This book is my sincere and honest effort to highlight the most pragmatic, basic, and easily ignored aspects of life during residency. Having done two heavy residencies, I understood how much life exists outside the white coat — and how deeply it impacts your whole being.

When I raised my voice on social media about NEET, residency toxicity, and work ethics — messages flooded in every day. Every struggle was different, yet the fundamentals were the same.

This book carries 6 years of wisdom, insights from 20+ successful doctors, and my own story — infused together so you can use it as a personal guide alongside your study books.

I hope it brings you clarity, grounds you in basics, and disciplines you toward the ideal version of you.
